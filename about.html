About Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against artery walls. Keeping it in a healthy range reduces risk of heart disease and stroke.

Key Numbers

Systolic is the top number; diastolic is the bottom. Aim for below 120/80 mmHg for most adults.

Why It Matters

High blood pressure strains the heart and vessels, increasing risks of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

How to Measure

Rest 5 minutes, sit upright, and use a validated upper-arm cuff. Take two readings and average them.
