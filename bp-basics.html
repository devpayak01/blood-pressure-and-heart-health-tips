Blood Pressure Basics

Know your systolic and diastolic numbers, categories, and how to measure correctly at home.

What the numbers mean

Systolic (top number) measures pressure when the heart beats. Diastolic (bottom number) measures pressure when the heart rests between beats.

Normal
< 120/80
Elevated
120-129/<80
Hypertension Stage 1
130-139 or 80-89
Hypertension Stage 2
≥ 140 or ≥ 90

How to measure at home

  • Sit with back supported, feet flat, and arm at heart level.
  • Avoid caffeine/exercise 30 minutes beforehand; rest 5 minutes.
  • Use a validated upper-arm cuff that fits your arm.
  • Take two readings 1 minute apart; record the average.

Quick Converter

mmHg to kPa (approx): multiply by 0.133.

Tip

Measure at the same times daily to see trends clearly.
